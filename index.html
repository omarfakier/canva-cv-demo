How to create a CV

  1. Log in to your Canva account. Start with a ready-made CV template, or begin with a blank design.
  2. Choose a layout that fits your goal. Pick from simple, professional, student, creative, or first-job CV templates.
  3. Add your details. Include your contact information, personal summary, education, work experience, skills, achievements, and references where relevant.
  4. Customise your CV. Change the colours, fonts, spacing, and layout so your CV looks clear, polished, and easy to read.
  5. Download your CV as a PDF, print it, or share it online when you are ready to apply.

Your questions, answered

  • You can create a CV for free by starting with a Canva CV template and customising it with your own details. Choose a layout, add your contact information, education, work experience, skills, and references, then download your CV as a PDF when you are ready to apply.

  • A strong CV usually includes your contact details, a short personal summary, education, work experience, skills, achievements, and references if required. If you have little or no work experience, you can include school projects, volunteer work, short courses, leadership roles, or other experience that shows your potential.

  • A CV should be clear, organised, and easy to read. Use simple headings, enough spacing, readable fonts, and a layout that helps employers quickly understand your skills and experience. Canva’s CV templates give you a professional structure so you do not have to design the layout from scratch.

  • Yes. Once your CV is ready, you can download it as a PDF, print it, or share it online. You can also return to Canva later to update your CV when your experience, skills, or qualifications change.
